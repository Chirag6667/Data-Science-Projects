🌍 Global Labor Market Analysis (1991--2025)
===========================================

This project provides a comprehensive Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) of global employment and unemployment trends spanning 35 years. By analyzing over **57,000 records**, this study uncovers how age, gender, and geography influence economic stability.

🚀 Project Overview
-------------------

Labor markets are the heartbeat of global stability. This project aims to visualize historical shifts---from the 2008 financial crisis to the 2020 pandemic---and evaluate the projected recovery through 2025.

🛠️ Technical Stack
-------------------

-   **Python 3.x**

-   **Pandas**: High-performance data manipulation and cleaning.

-   **Matplotlib & Seaborn**: Statistical data visualization and time-series plotting.

-   **NumPy**: Numerical operations for trend analysis.

📈 Analysis Highlights
----------------------

### 1\. Global Trend Mapping

-   Normalized 35 years of data to identify macro-economic cycles.

-   Identified the **9.4%** unemployment floor projected for 2025.

### 2\. Demographic Deep-Dives

-   **Age Analysis:** Compared the "Youth vs. Adult" gap, revealing that the 15--24 age group faces significantly higher volatility.

-   **Gender Analysis:** Visualized the "Employment-to-Population" ratio to track the slow convergence of male and female labor participation.

### 3\. Geographic Extremes (2025 Projections)

-   Identified the Top 10 countries with the highest projected unemployment.

-   **Leader in Crisis:** Eswatini (34.2%) and South Africa (32.4%).

📂 Dataset Structure
--------------------

The analysis uses two symmetrical datasets:

-   `employment_rate.csv`: 57,519 rows tracking labor participation.

-   `unemployment_rate.csv`: 57,519 rows tracking joblessness.
